# Brand Identity Deep Dive

Use this before brainstorming names. The goal is to understand the culture of the product being built, not just the function.

## Read the product like a brand consultant

Pull from:
- interface design
- page hierarchy
- copy tone
- motion and visual restraint
- onboarding flow
- user outcome
- competitive position

Do not summarize the app as a feature list. Diagnose what kind of company this wants to be in the world.

## Questions to answer

1. What emotional state is the user in before they arrive?
2. What does the product help them feel one minute later?
3. What kind of competence does the UI project?
4. What cultural world does the product belong to?
5. What adjacent brands feel native to this product?
6. What kinds of names would immediately feel off-brand?

## Example read

A warm-but-precise property tax appeal product for homeowners might read as:
- competent, not playful
- relieving, not hype-driven
- evidence-based, not black-box magical
- financially helpful, not speculative

That should push naming toward fairness, proof, savings, or advocacy language and away from crypto, dev-tool, or abstract AI branding.
