# TLD Positioning

Use this when the naming work is blocked on "which TLD should lead the brand?"

## Default hierarchy

1. `.com`
2. `.ai` for AI-native products when the market expects it
3. `.io` for developer-facing or startup-native B2B tools
4. `.net` mainly as a defensive registration

## Marketing interpretation

### `.com`
- Broadest trust
- Best spoken recall
- Best default for paid acquisition, word of mouth, and mainstream audiences
- Strongest long-term asset if the company could become the category brand

### `.ai`
- Signals technical novelty and model-centric value
- Helpful when AI is the point of purchase, not just implementation detail
- Can reduce trust when the buyer wants reliability, compliance, or household familiarity more than novelty

### `.io`
- Reads startup, builder, or developer-first
- Works well for tools bought by technical teams
- Often feels niche or unfamiliar to mainstream consumers

### `.net`
- Feels legacy or secondary unless the company is already established there
- Fine as a defensive registration
- Rarely the right primary brand today

## Heuristic

Ask:
1. Would the user confidently type and trust this domain after hearing it once?
2. Does the TLD help the story, or force you to explain it?
3. If the company gets larger than its current wedge, will the TLD still fit?

If the answer to any of those is weak, keep looking.
